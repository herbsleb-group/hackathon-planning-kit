--- layout: archive --- This hackathon planning kit is the collective effort of a team of researchers at the Institute for Software Research in Carnegie Mellon's School of Computer Science.

This planning kit involves guidance for three of the most common types of hackathons: communal, contributive, and catalytic, which was constructed based on our empirical study of 14 hackathons including a large-scale corporate hackathon by Microsoft, events hosted by universities, and events hosted by scientific communities including three hack days at STScI.
