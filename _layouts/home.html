--- layout: archive --- We are a team of researchers at the Institute for Software Research in Carnegie Mellon's School of Computer Science.

Based on our empirical study of 14 hackathons including a large-scale corporate hackathon by Microsoft, events hosted by universities, and events hosted by scientific communities including three hack days at STScI, we provide a hackahton planning kit for three of the most common types of hackathons: communal, contributive, and catalytic.
